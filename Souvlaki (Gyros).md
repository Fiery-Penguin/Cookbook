#Bread 
[[Cookbook|Return]] 
## Ingredients (2 Gyros)
___
**Meat**
- [ ] 1 chunk of pork or chicken cut into strips
- [ ] 2 Tbsp Dried Oregano
- [ ] 2 Tbsp Dried Thyme
- [ ] 1 Tbsp Dried Parsley
- [ ] 1 Tbsp Garlic Powder
- [ ] 1 Tbsp Ground Cumin
- [ ] 1 Tbsp Paprika Powder
- [ ] 1 Tbsp Coriander
- [ ] 1 Tbsp Chili powder (or 1 Tsp Cayenne)
- [ ] 1/2 Tbsp Black Pepper
- [ ] 1/2 Tbsp Ground Cinnamon
- [ ] 1/2 Tbsp Onion Powder
- [ ] 1/2 Tbsp Salt

**Bread**
- [ ] 200 g Flour
- [ ] 100 g water
- [ ] 20 g oil
- [ ] 4 g salt

**Tzatziki**
- [ ] 1/2 Cucumber
- [ ] 3 large Tbsp Greek yogurt
- [ ] 1 clove of Garlic
- [ ] 1 squirt of lemon juice
- [ ] Salt to taste

**Filling**
- [ ] 2 Tomatoes, cubed
- [ ] 1 Red Onion, Sliced
- [ ] 1 Block of Feta, Cut into 1-cm cubes
- [ ] 300 g Curly Fries
## Walkthrough
___
**Meat Marinade**
1. Cut the meat into very thin slices, possibly freeze the meat before hand to make it easier.
2. Mix all the different spices together with a little bit of oil and mix the spices well into the meat, making sure it gets unto every surface, set aside and let rest.
3. Once it's time to cook the meat, Heat up a pan with a bit of oil. Once hot, add the meat and let it sit in the pan a little bit, constantly stirring will prevent nice browning, but make sure the meat doesn't burn.
4. The meat doesn't need long because it's cut so thinly, so after a few minutes, cut the heat and enjoy.

**Bread**
1. Add all the ingredients together in a bowl and mix well to combine, put out on the table and knead for a good 5 minutes, form into a ball and rest for a 10 minutes covered to let the gluten relax and make it easier to shape. Preheat the oven with an oven tray at the medium position to $250\degree C$.
2. Once rested, cut into 2 equal size balls and roll one into a large square, rest it a few minutes before putting it into the oven to prevent the tight gluten from pulling the dough together.
3. Bake the dough for about 3 minutes, flip the dough and check for colour, once it's a little spotted but not crisp at all, it's done. Take out and let rest under a towel to keep the moisture in. Continue this for the rest of the bread.

**Tzatziki**
1. Cut the Cucumber into strips using a grater and squeeze as much water out as possible to prevent a watery tzatziki, Grate the garlic finely and add the cucumber and garlic to a bowl with the yogurt, add salt to taste.

**Building**
1. Once everything is prepared, add a layer of tzatziki to the bottom of the bread, add slices of onion, tomato, potatoes and feta, finishing with the meat and a bit more tzatziki for good measure, Gyros are not meant to be able to be rolled like a shawarma, so just fold it and maybe cover in a bit of baking paper (or tin foil if you're cringe). Enjoy!

***Order of operations***
*This recipe has a lot of parallel cooking. Being smart about the timings will allow the dish to come together very quickly. If you're doing the potatoes from scratch, those will take the longest, so start baking the potatoes, prepare the dough, marinade the meat, and while waiting on the potatoes, make the tzatziki. Once the potatoes are done, use the heat from the oven to cook the bread, turn on the heat for the meat, and cook that once the bread has been made and is resting under a towel. This we've found to be the best order of operations.*
